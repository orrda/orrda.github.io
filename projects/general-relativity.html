Einstein Field Equations

Deriving the field equations from the Einstein–Hilbert action and solving them in symmetric special cases.

The Einstein–Hilbert action

General relativity follows from varying the Einstein–Hilbert action with respect to the metric $g_{\mu\nu}$:

$$S = \frac{1}{16\pi G}\int \left(R - 2\Lambda\right)\sqrt{-g}\,d^4x + S_{\text{matter}}$$

The field equations

Setting $\delta S / \delta g^{\mu\nu} = 0$ yields the Einstein field equations:

$$G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G\, T_{\mu\nu}$$

where $G_{\mu\nu} = R_{\mu\nu} - \tfrac{1}{2}R g_{\mu\nu}$ is the Einstein tensor and $T_{\mu\nu}$ is the stress-energy tensor.

Schwarzschild solution

For a static, spherically symmetric vacuum ($T_{\mu\nu} = 0$, $\Lambda = 0$) the unique solution is the Schwarzschild metric:

$$ds^2 = -\!\left(1 - \frac{2GM}{r}\right)c^2\,dt^2 + \left(1 - \frac{2GM}{r}\right)^{\!-1}\!dr^2 + r^2\,d\Omega^2$$

The Schwarzschild radius $r_s = 2GM/c^2$ marks the event horizon of a non-rotating black hole.

FLRW cosmology

Assuming a homogeneous, isotropic universe the metric takes the Friedmann–Lemaître–Robertson–Walker form, and Einstein's equations reduce to the Friedmann equations:

$$\left(\frac{\dot{a}}{a}\right)^2 = \frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho - \frac{kc^2}{a^2} + \frac{\Lambda c^2}{3}$$

where $a(t)$ is the scale factor and $k \in \{-1, 0, +1\}$ is the spatial curvature.

Mass Transfer in Close Binaries

Mass transfer in close binaries is commonly modeled as an isentropic outflow through the first Lagrange point ($L_1$), yet under sufficiently rapid overflow material can also escape through the outer Lagrange point ($L_2$). This thesis develops an analytic framework for quantifying the mass-loss rate and predicting the fraction of the total outflow that is ejected via $L_2$.

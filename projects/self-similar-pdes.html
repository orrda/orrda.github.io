Self-similar Solutions to Non-linear PDEs

Exploiting scaling symmetries to reduce non-linear PDEs to ODEs and find Barenblatt-type self-similar profiles.

Scaling symmetry

A PDE admits a scaling symmetry if the transformation $(t, x, u) \mapsto (\lambda^\alpha t,\, \lambda^\beta x,\, \lambda^\gamma u)$ maps solutions to solutions for every $\lambda > 0$. Self-similar solutions then take the form

$$u(x,t) = t^{-\gamma/\alpha}\, U\!\left(\frac{x}{t^{\beta/\alpha}}\right)$$

where the similarity variable $\xi = x\,t^{-\beta/\alpha}$ reduces the PDE to an ODE for the profile $U(\xi)$.

Porous medium equation

The porous medium equation $u_t = \Delta(u^m)$, $m > 1$, admits the Barenblatt solution

$$u(x,t) = t^{-k}\!\left(C - \frac{m-1}{2m}\,\frac{|x|^2}{t^{2k/n}}\right)_+^{1/(m-1)}, \quad k = \frac{n}{n(m-1)+2}$$

This is a self-similar solution of the second kind whose compact support expands as $t^{k/n}$, exhibiting finite-speed propagation — in contrast to the linear heat equation.

Non-linear heat equation and blow-up

For the focusing non-linear heat equation $u_t = \Delta u + u^p$, $p > 1$, blow-up in finite time $T^*$ can occur. Near blow-up the solution concentrates on a self-similar profile:

$$u(x,t) \approx (T^* - t)^{-1/(p-1)}\,\Phi\!\left(\frac{x}{\sqrt{T^*-t}}\right)$$

The profile $\Phi$ satisfies a non-linear elliptic ODE; proving its existence and stability is a central problem in the field.

Lie symmetry analysis

Systematic reduction is provided by Lie's classical method. For a PDE $F[u] = 0$ admitting infinitesimal generator $\mathbf{v} = \xi^i(x,u)\partial_{x^i} + \phi(x,u)\partial_u$, the invariance condition $\mathrm{pr}^{(k)}\mathbf{v}(F)\big|_{F=0} = 0$ yields an overdetermined linear system for $\xi^i$ and $\phi$.

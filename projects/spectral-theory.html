Spectral Theory of Compact Operators

Spectral decomposition of compact self-adjoint operators on Hilbert spaces, with applications to Fredholm integral equations.

Overview

The spectral theorem for compact self-adjoint operators is one of the cornerstones of functional analysis. It says that every compact self-adjoint operator $T : H \to H$ on a Hilbert space $H$ admits an orthonormal basis of eigenvectors whose corresponding eigenvalues form a sequence converging to zero.

Key definitions

An operator $T : H \to H$ is compact if it maps bounded sets to relatively compact sets. It is self-adjoint if $\langle Tx, y\rangle = \langle x, Ty\rangle$ for all $x, y \in H$.

$$\sigma(T) \setminus \{0\} = \{\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \ldots\}, \quad \lambda_n \to 0 \text{ as } n \to \infty$$

Spectral Theorem (compact self-adjoint)

Let $T$ be a compact self-adjoint operator on an infinite-dimensional separable Hilbert space $H$. There exists a countable orthonormal set $\{e_n\}$ of eigenvectors and a corresponding sequence of real eigenvalues $\{\lambda_n\}$ such that:

  • $T e_n = \lambda_n e_n$ for all $n$
  • $|\lambda_1| \geq |\lambda_2| \geq \cdots \geq 0$
  • $\lambda_n \to 0$
  • $T x = \sum_{n} \lambda_n \langle x, e_n \rangle e_n$ for all $x \in H$
$$T = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda_n \langle \cdot,\, e_n \rangle\, e_n$$

Application: Fredholm integral equations

A Fredholm integral equation of the second kind,

$$\phi(x) - \mu \int_a^b K(x,t)\,\phi(t)\,dt = f(x),$$

where $K \in L^2([a,b]^2)$ is a symmetric kernel, can be solved via the eigenfunction expansion of the associated compact self-adjoint operator $(T\phi)(x) = \int_a^b K(x,t)\phi(t)\,dt$.

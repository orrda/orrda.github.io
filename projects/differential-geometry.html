Riemannian Geometry & Curvature

Riemannian manifolds, the curvature tensor, geodesics, and the bridge to Einstein's theory of gravity.

Riemannian manifolds

A Riemannian manifold $(M, g)$ is a smooth manifold $M$ equipped with a smooth, symmetric, positive-definite $(0,2)$-tensor field $g$ (the metric). The metric assigns an inner product to each tangent space $T_p M$.

The Levi-Civita connection

There is a unique torsion-free metric-compatible connection $\nabla$ (the Levi-Civita connection) whose Christoffel symbols are

$$\Gamma^\sigma_{\mu\nu} = \frac{1}{2}g^{\sigma\rho} \!\left(\partial_\mu g_{\nu\rho} + \partial_\nu g_{\mu\rho} - \partial_\rho g_{\mu\nu}\right)$$

Geodesics

A curve $\gamma : I \to M$ is a geodesic if it parallel-transports its own tangent vector:

$$\frac{d^2 x^\sigma}{d\tau^2} + \Gamma^\sigma_{\mu\nu} \frac{dx^\mu}{d\tau}\frac{dx^\nu}{d\tau} = 0$$

Geodesics locally minimise (or extremise) the length functional and generalise the notion of straight lines to curved spaces.

The Riemann curvature tensor

The Riemann tensor measures the failure of second covariant derivatives to commute:

$$R^\rho{}_{\sigma\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu\Gamma^\rho_{\nu\sigma} - \partial_\nu\Gamma^\rho_{\mu\sigma} + \Gamma^\rho_{\mu\lambda}\Gamma^\lambda_{\nu\sigma} - \Gamma^\rho_{\nu\lambda}\Gamma^\lambda_{\mu\sigma}$$

Ricci tensor and scalar curvature

The Ricci tensor $R_{\mu\nu} = R^\rho{}_{\mu\rho\nu}$ and the Ricci scalar $R = g^{\mu\nu}R_{\mu\nu}$ are key contractions used in Einstein's field equations.

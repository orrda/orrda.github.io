Analytic “AND” Function

Constructing real-analytic and polynomial approximations to the Boolean AND function, with implications for circuit complexity and learning theory.

The Boolean AND function

The $n$-bit AND function $\mathrm{AND}_n : \{0,1\}^n \to \{0,1\}$ outputs $1$ if and only if every input bit equals $1$. Equivalently, on $\{-1,+1\}^n$ with the convention $1 \leftrightarrow +1$ and $0 \leftrightarrow -1$,

$$\mathrm{AND}_n(x_1,\ldots,x_n) = \frac{1}{2}\!\left(1 + \prod_{i=1}^n x_i\right) \cdot \prod_{i=1}^n \frac{1+x_i}{2}$$

The multilinear representation is the unique multilinear polynomial agreeing with AND on all Boolean inputs.

Fourier analysis on the Boolean cube

Every $f : \{-1,+1\}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ has a unique multilinear expansion (Fourier–Walsh expansion):

$$f(x) = \sum_{S \subseteq [n]} \hat{f}(S)\,\chi_S(x), \qquad \chi_S(x) = \prod_{i \in S} x_i$$

For $\mathrm{AND}_n$ the Fourier coefficients are $\hat{\mathrm{AND}}_n(S) = (-1)^{n-|S|} / 2^n$ for all $S \subseteq [n]$, giving total influence $\mathbf{I}[\mathrm{AND}_n] = n/2^{n-1}$.

Analytic approximation

We seek a real-analytic function $F : \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ that (i) agrees with AND on $\{0,1\}^n$, (ii) is smooth everywhere, and (iii) has controlled growth outside the unit cube $[0,1]^n$. One natural candidate uses the sigmoid:

$$F_\beta(x) = \sigma\!\left(\beta\!\left(\prod_{i=1}^n x_i - \tfrac{1}{2}\right)\right), \qquad \sigma(t) = \frac{1}{1+e^{-t}}$$

As $\beta \to \infty$, $F_\beta$ converges pointwise to AND on $\{0,1\}^n$, but the gradient concentrates near the boundary of the Boolean cube.

Degree and approximation complexity

The approximate degree $\widetilde{\deg}_\varepsilon(f)$ is the minimum degree of a real polynomial $p$ satisfying $\|p - f\|_\infty \leq \varepsilon$ over $\{0,1\}^n$. It is known that

$$\widetilde{\deg}_{1/3}(\mathrm{AND}_n) = \Theta(\sqrt{n})$$

matching the lower bound from quantum query complexity via the polynomial method (Beals–Buhrman–Cleve–Mosca–de Wolf).

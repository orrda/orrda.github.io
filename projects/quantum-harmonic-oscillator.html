Quantum Harmonic Oscillator

Ladder operators, coherent states, and path integral treatment of the fundamental quantum system.

The Hamiltonian

The quantum harmonic oscillator is defined by the Hamiltonian

$$\hat{H} = \frac{\hat{p}^2}{2m} + \frac{1}{2}m\omega^2 \hat{x}^2$$

where $[\hat{x}, \hat{p}] = i\hbar$.

Ladder operators

We introduce the annihilation and creation operators

$$\hat{a} = \sqrt{\frac{m\omega}{2\hbar}}\!\left(\hat{x} + \frac{i\hat{p}}{m\omega}\right), \qquad \hat{a}^\dagger = \sqrt{\frac{m\omega}{2\hbar}}\!\left(\hat{x} - \frac{i\hat{p}}{m\omega}\right)$$

satisfying $[\hat{a}, \hat{a}^\dagger] = 1$, so that $\hat{H} = \hbar\omega\!\left(\hat{a}^\dagger\hat{a} + \tfrac{1}{2}\right)$.

Energy spectrum

The energy eigenstates $|n\rangle$ satisfy

$$E_n = \hbar\omega\!\left(n + \tfrac{1}{2}\right), \quad n = 0, 1, 2, \ldots$$

The ground state $|0\rangle$ is annihilated by $\hat{a}$: $\hat{a}|0\rangle = 0$, giving the Gaussian wave function $\psi_0(x) = \left(\frac{m\omega}{\pi\hbar}\right)^{1/4} e^{-m\omega x^2 / 2\hbar}$.

Coherent states

Coherent states $|\alpha\rangle$ are eigenstates of $\hat{a}$: $\hat{a}|\alpha\rangle = \alpha|\alpha\rangle$, $\alpha \in \mathbb{C}$. They minimise the Heisenberg uncertainty and form an overcomplete basis for the Fock space.

$$|\alpha\rangle = e^{-|\alpha|^2/2} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{\alpha^n}{\sqrt{n!}}|n\rangle$$

Path integral formulation

The propagator $K(x_f, t_f; x_i, t_i)$ can be computed as a Feynman path integral, yielding the exact result

$$K = \sqrt{\frac{m\omega}{2\pi i\hbar\sin\omega T}} \exp\!\left(\frac{im\omega}{2\hbar\sin\omega T} \bigl[(x_f^2 + x_i^2)\cos\omega T - 2x_f x_i\bigr]\right)$$

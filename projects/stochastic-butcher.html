Stochastic Butcher Matrices

Extending Butcher's B-series and Runge–Kutta tableaux to stochastic differential equations, and deriving the resulting order conditions.

Classical Butcher tableaux

A Runge–Kutta method for the ODE $\dot{y} = f(y)$ is encoded by a Butcher tableau $(A, b, c)$ where $A \in \mathbb{R}^{s\times s}$, $b \in \mathbb{R}^s$, $c = A\mathbf{1}$:

$$k_i = f\!\left(y_n + h\sum_{j=1}^s a_{ij} k_j\right), \qquad y_{n+1} = y_n + h \sum_{i=1}^s b_i k_i$$

The order conditions — algebraic equations that $A$ and $b$ must satisfy for a given order $p$ — are elegantly indexed by rooted trees via Butcher's B-series.

Itô SDEs and Stratonovich form

Consider the Itô SDE $dX = f(X)\,dt + g(X)\,dW$ where $W$ is a Brownian motion. In Stratonovich form $dX = \tilde{f}(X)\circ dt + g(X)\circ dW$ with $\tilde{f} = f - \tfrac{1}{2}g'g$, the chain rule takes its classical form, making the algebra of stochastic methods more transparent.

Stochastic Runge–Kutta methods

A stochastic Runge–Kutta (SRK) method approximates $X(t_{n+1})$ by evaluating $f$ and $g$ at multiple stages. For Itô order 1.5 (strong) the method requires two Butcher-like matrices $(A^{(0)}, A^{(1)})$ corresponding to the drift and diffusion evaluations:

$$H_i^{(0)} = X_n + \sum_j a_{ij}^{(0)} f(H_j^{(0)})\,h + \sum_j a_{ij}^{(1)} g(H_j^{(1)})\,\Delta W$$

Stochastic B-series and order conditions

Burrage and Burrage (1996) extended B-series to SDEs by replacing rooted trees with coloured trees whose nodes are labelled by the differential operator applied (drift or diffusion). The strong order-$p$ conditions take the form

$$\Phi(t) = \frac{1}{\gamma(t)}, \quad \forall\, t \in T^{(S)}, |t| \leq p$$

where $\gamma(t)$ is the density of the tree and $\Phi(t)$ is a polynomial in the entries of $A^{(0)}, A^{(1)}, b^{(0)}, b^{(1)}$.
